Electrical Engineering — Stanford University

Hi, I’m James.

I am a second-year Electrical Engineering major at Stanford University, with a potential focus on the Hardware and Software track. I have a strong interest in circuitry, particularly in how simple circuits can be expanded and integrated into more complex systems that power the technologies we interact with every day. Through my coursework, I have also seen how computer science concepts intersect with electrical engineering, especially in the classes that I take. I am extremely interested in being a part of a team that develops technological advancements that improve humanity.

Some facts about me are that I was born and raised in Los Angeles, CA. I am the youngest of three siblings to a Guatemalan father and a Mexican mother. Outside of labs and classes, I am huge fanatic of soccer and of Real Madrid and the Lakers, my home team. I am a black belt in Shito-Ryu Karate and have been practicing for over a decade (°O°). Much more recently, I have gotten into running and learning how to play the guitar. Thanks for reading!

Stanford University

Relevant Coursework

A selection of technical courses that have shaped my skills in engineering, systems, and programming.

CS 107 Computer Science

Computer Organization & Systems

Systems-focused course using C to understand how programs run on real hardware, covering data representation, pointers, memory allocation, machine-level code, and a final project implementing custom heap allocators.

C x86-64 Assembly GDB Memory Management
CS 106B Computer Science

Programming Abstractions

Second course in Stanford’s CS intro sequence using C++ to explore data structures, recursion, memory management, and linked structures, designing algorithms and implementing abstractions like queues and doubly linked lists.

C++ Data Structures Algorithms Recursion
EE 102A Electrical Engineering

Signal Processing & Linear Systems I

Introductory course on continuous- and discrete-time signals and systems, covering LTI modeling, convolution, Fourier series/transforms, sampling and filtering, with MATLAB labs to simulate and analyze real-world signals.

MATLAB Fourier Analysis Filter Design LTI Systems DT Signals CS Signals
EE 101A Electrical Engineering

Circuits I

Studied circuit modeling and analysis, from resistive and diode networks to MOSFET amplifiers, applying nodal and mesh analysis, Kirchhoff’s laws, and related methods (e.g., superposition, Thevenin/Norton), and built a multi-stage AC–DC converter.

Circuit Analysis Op-Amps AC/DC SPICE
ENGR 40M Engineering

An Intro to Making: What is EE?

Introduction to electrical engineering covering core circuit and signal concepts through hands-on projects (useless box, LED display, ECG), focusing on circuits, microcontroller programming, and debugging hardware–software interactions.

Soldering Microcontrollers Schematics Breadboards
