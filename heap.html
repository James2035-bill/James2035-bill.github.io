In the final assignment of CS 107, I implemented two heap allocators — implicit and explicit.

Implicit Allocator

The implicit allocator traverses every node in the heap to find free blocks, resulting in an O(n) mymalloc and O(n) myrealloc, with myfree running in O(1). Simple to implement, but inefficient at scale.

Explicit Allocator

The explicit allocator improves on this by maintaining a doubly linked list of only free nodes, so mymalloc skips allocated blocks entirely — reducing search time significantly. myrealloc also supports in-place reallocation by checking whether neighboring free nodes can be absorbed to satisfy the request. myfree remains O(1).
